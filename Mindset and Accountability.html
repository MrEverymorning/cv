Mindset and Accountability

a short essay


Accountability is reporting on fulfilling my responsibilities to the appropriate people. For example, I always report to my wife what I've learned or done in a day. Partly that's because I take everyday seriously. When I see my wife at the end of a day, I impulsively feel a desire to share my day with her, my successes and failures, and my goals for tomorrow.

I've gotten thirty five star ratings on my little business. That because I follow through, I keep in contact if I'm behind schedule, and I always make sure I know, and the client knows, what needs to be done, how it's going to be done, and what happens if a variable arises.

Mindset. What is my mindset? I love the daoist tradition of eating only until you are 80% full. In my own experience, life is always better if we allow ourselves to be both grateful and hungry. Life is not about indulgence or waiting to hit it big.

If I'm too hungry for success, I can get overwhelmed. Success often comes slow, and with patience and consistency. Life is more of a marathon than a sprint.

However, if I'm too content, I'll become stagnant, comfortable, and unprepared for challenges. I'll start to become irritable, or lazy, complacent, or redundant.

I code eight hours a day, but I take breaks every 25 minutes to do yoga, take a walk. Sometimes I do nothing for thirty minutes, meditating. Sometimes I'll be coding and I'll get a spiritual thought. Sometimes I'm with my wife. But I believe that amidst all of this, it's important to listen to my feelings, and never overwhelm myself, nor to loosen the cords on my discipline.
